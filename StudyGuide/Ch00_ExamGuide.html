CSG drill — the emphasized one

EmphasisExamCard 4

Exam: DE-Q44 · MP-Q4 — "complete the CSG operation" — the prof stressed this (book p.33).

① Read the boolean from the result (two squares A & B)

② Redraw the CSG tree: booleans at nodes, primitives at leaves

What CSG is the implicit solid-modeling approach: a set of primitives (box, cylinder, sphere, cone…) combined by Boolean operations (union, intersection, difference), stored as a binary tree (procedural construction history).

Why it matters implicit → traceable + easy to modify + small files, but the receiver must re-evaluate it (contrast: explicit B-rep). Modern CAD applies booleans to any volume, not just primitives.

Exam answerGiven a figure: merged = Union, overlap-only = Intersection, A-with-B-removed = Difference. Given a tree: internal nodes are boolean operators, leaves are primitives; evaluate bottom-up. CSG belongs to the implicit approach (Ch 2).

Prof emphasis: the exact CSG tree from the BIM Book (p.33) has appeared without the operators, asking you to fill in ∪ / ∩ / −. Practice filling operators into a blank tree.

Exam-day checklist

1Pace: ~1 pt/min. Do the definitions and single-choice first (fast points), then the sketch/long ones.
2Sketches: if asked to draw, it's a Processes / Structures / Relationships diagram (Card 3) — or a CSG figure (Card 4).
3Single-choice traps: IFC "wrong statement" = Property Sets restricted to schema (false); energy formats = gbXML + IFC4; UML vs BPMN.
4Two different LODs: building LOD 100–500 (Ch 10) vs CityGML LOD 0–4 (Ch 9) — don't mix.
5BIM Book: a few items are book-only (open/closed BIM p.12, CSG tree p.33, IFC layers) — covered in Ch 1 / 2 / 3.
