Chapter 9 — BIM & GIS (3D City Models)

Scaling up from the building to the city. GIS/city models describe urban areas via surfaces (CityGML), which differ fundamentally from IFC's volume model — and use their own 5-level Level of Detail.

CityGML vs IFC — paradigm

CompareExamCard 2

Exam: DE-Q18 — data-paradigm difference — asked repeatedly

Exam answerIFC = element-based volume (solid) model; CityGML = surface model. IFC builds up from constructive elements; CityGML describes observed surfaces.

3D city model · CityGML · XPlanung

DefinitionExamCard 3

Exam: DE-Q19 — define 3D city model + components; CityGML; XPlanung

3D city model digital model of urban areas. Components: buildings, vegetation, water bodies, transportation, terrain, city furniture.

CityGML City Geography Markup Language — open standard to store/transfer virtual 3D city models (covers geometry, topology, semantics, appearance).

XPlanung open standard for digital exchange/archiving of land-use, landscape & regional plans (2D semantic).

Exam answer3D city model = digital urban model (buildings, vegetation, water…). CityGML = open 3D-city standard. XPlanung = standard for land-use/regional plans.

City-model Level of Detail (LOD 0–4)

SketchExamCard 4

Exam: DE-Q21 — list & describe the LODs — asked repeatedly

CityGML 2.0 · 5 levels (CityGML 3.0 removes LOD4)

Exam answerLOD0 terrain (2.5D) · LOD1 block, no roof · LOD2 + roof/texture · LOD3 detailed architectural · LOD4 walkable interiors.

Watch out: this is the CityGML LOD (0–4), NOT the building LOD 100–500 in Ch 10.
